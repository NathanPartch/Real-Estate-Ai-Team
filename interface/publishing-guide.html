Path A — Free

List on Zillow as FSBO

Takes about 20 minutes. Your listing appears on Zillow and Trulia. Active for 90 days (free to renew).

⚠️
Zillow FSBO listings are NOT on the MLS. Buyers working with agents may never see your home unless you also do a flat fee MLS listing. Consider Path B alongside this one.
1

Create a Zillow account

Go to zillow.com and click Sign In → New Account in the top right. You can sign up with Google, Facebook, Apple, or email. Use the email address you want buyers to contact you at.

2

Find and claim your home

Type your address in the search bar. On your home's Zestimate page, click More → Claim ownership. Zillow will verify you own it — this takes up to 72 hours. You'll get an email when it's confirmed.

3

Go to your Owner Dashboard and start the listing

Once claimed, click your profile icon → My Zillow → My Homes. Find your property and click List Home for Sale By Owner.

4

Set your price

Enter your asking price. Zillow will show you the Zestimate for reference — you don't have to match it, but be ready to explain the difference to buyers. Your listing agent's market analysis has your recommended range.

5

Upload your photos

Upload at minimum 15–25 photos. Lead with the front exterior, then kitchen, living room, primary bedroom, bathrooms, backyard. Zillow sorts by first photo — make it your best. Photos must be horizontal (landscape orientation), well-lit, and clutter-free.

  • Minimum recommended: 15 photos
  • Ideal: 25–40 photos including all rooms, exterior, and street view
  • File size: under 10MB each, JPEG or PNG
  • If you used AI to edit or virtually stage any photo: California law (effective Jan 2026) requires you to disclose this and keep the original unedited version
6

Fill in your home facts

Enter all details accurately. Zillow will pre-fill some from public records — review every field and correct anything wrong. Fields to complete:

  • Bedrooms, bathrooms (full and half separately)
  • Square footage (living area)
  • Lot size
  • Year built
  • Garage type and capacity
  • HOA: yes/no, monthly amount
  • Parking, laundry, heating/cooling type
7

Paste in your listing description

Use the description your listing agent prepared. This goes in the Home Description field. Keep it under 700 characters for the MLS-facing fields, but Zillow allows longer — paste the full marketing description here.

8

Add your contact information

Enter the phone number and email you want buyers to call/text/email directly. This is visible to the public — use a number you're comfortable sharing. Consider a Google Voice number if you prefer privacy.

9

Agree to terms and submit

Check the Terms of Use box and click Post for Sale by Owner. Your listing will go live within a few hours. You'll receive a confirmation email with a link to your live listing.

10

Renew every 90 days

Zillow FSBO listings expire after 90 days. You'll get an email reminder before it expires. Log in and click Extend Listing — it's free and takes 30 seconds. Do this every 90 days until sold.

2

Decide what you'll offer a buyer's agent — before we list

This is one of the most important decisions you'll make before going live. Here's what it means and why it matters:

What a buyer's agent commission is: When a buyer uses a real estate agent to help them purchase a home, that agent expects to be paid a commission — typically a percentage of the sale price. As the seller, you can choose to offer to cover this fee, or not. This choice directly affects how many agents are willing to bring their buyers to see your home.
Offer 2%–2.5% → Most showings
This is the most common offer. Agents actively search for homes that offer a buyer's commission, so your listing will appear in more agent searches. More agents showing your home = more offers = more competition = better final price. The fee comes out of your sale proceeds at closing — you don't pay it upfront.
Offer 1%–1.5% → Fewer agent showings, more savings
Some agents will still show your home. You'll save more at closing but may see less agent-driven interest. Works better if your home is priced well below market or if the market is very hot.
Offer 0% → Direct buyers only
See Step 3 — this is worth understanding before deciding. Direct buyers (no agent) can still purchase your home and do not require any commission from you.

Our recommendation for first-time sellers: Start at 2%–2.5%. You can always negotiate down during an offer — but you can't attract showings you never got.

3

If an agent approaches you directly — know this

Sometimes a buyer's agent will contact you directly about your listing — by phone, email, or at an open house. This is normal. Here is what to expect and how to handle it:

What they'll ask about: The agent will likely ask whether you're offering a buyer's agent commission and how much. This is a standard question — they need to know how their client (the buyer) will cover their fee.
  • If you listed a commission on the MLS: Tell them the amount you listed. For example: "We're offering 2.5% to the buyer's agent, as stated in the MLS." That's all you need to say.
  • If they ask for more than you listed: You don't have to agree. The amount you set is your offer. You can say "Our offer is 2.5% — that's what we're working with." Negotiating commission is normal, but so is holding your position.
  • If you listed 0% and an agent calls: Be upfront: "We're not offering a buyer's agent commission. The buyer would need to cover your fee separately." Some agents will proceed — especially if their buyer really wants your property. Some won't.
  • Never verbally agree to a commission you haven't put in writing. Any commission agreement should be in the purchase contract — not a phone conversation.
Tip: Before your listing goes live, decide on a number you're comfortable with and write it down. When an agent calls, you'll have a clear, confident answer ready instead of being caught off guard.
4

Direct buyers — no commission required

A direct buyer is someone who finds your home and contacts you without using a real estate agent. They may find you through Zillow, Facebook, a yard sign, or word of mouth.

Direct buyers do not require any commission from you. There is no agent involved, so there is no agent to pay. This is one of the biggest financial advantages of selling FSBO — if a buyer comes directly to you, you keep that percentage in your pocket.
  • Direct buyers have full access to your listing — they can see everything on Zillow, Realtor.com, and Redfin, and can contact you directly using the number on your listing.
  • You negotiate the price and terms directly with them, without a commission middleman.
  • They may still hire a real estate attorney to review the contract — that's their cost, not yours.
  • You should still use a title company or escrow officer to handle the closing paperwork safely. That fee (typically 0.5%–1% of the sale price, split between buyer and seller) applies to all transactions regardless of how the buyer found you.

Where inquiries go — and what you can control

This is one of the most important things to understand before your listing goes live. Each platform handles buyer inquiries differently, and none of them send everything directly to you by default. Here's exactly what happens on each one:

MLS → goes to our brokerage, forwarded to you
Inquiries that come through the MLS listing go to us as the listing broker. We forward every one to you immediately by email and text. This is reliable and fast.
Zillow & Trulia → goes to you directly (as listing broker)
Because we submit your listing through a licensed brokerage, our contact information appears on Zillow as the listing agent. We forward every Zillow inquiry to you the same day. Direct buyers who find the listing can also contact us directly, and we pass them to you immediately.
Realtor.com → partially
Realtor.com often routes inquiries to agents who pay for lead placement in your zip code — not the listing agent. We receive some inquiries and forward them, but Realtor.com may also send the same lead to other agents. You won't always get every Realtor.com contact. This is a platform limitation we can't change.
Redfin → does not forward to sellers or outside brokers
Redfin is its own brokerage. When a buyer clicks "Contact Agent" on Redfin, the inquiry goes to a Redfin agent — not to us and not to you. There is no way to change this. Redfin's value is in the exposure it creates, not the direct leads. Buyers who are serious will still find your contact info on Zillow or call the number on your yard sign.
The most reliable path to your inbox: Make sure your phone number is on your yard sign and clearly visible in your Zillow listing description. Most serious buyers — both agent-represented and direct — will call or text the number they can find. That goes straight to you, no platform routing involved.
Path C — Free

Free marketing — do these regardless

These cost nothing and move the needle. Do all of them after your listing is live.

1

Post on Facebook Marketplace

Go to facebook.com/marketplace → click Sell → Homes for Sale or Rent. Add your photos, price, description, and contact info. This is the #1 free channel for direct buyer inquiries. Facebook Marketplace listings are also indexed by Google.

2

Post in local Facebook groups

Search Facebook for "[Your City] Real Estate" or "[Your Neighborhood] Community" groups. Join and post your listing with 3–5 photos and a short description. These groups often have thousands of local members actively looking.

3

Share on Instagram and Nextdoor

Post 5–8 of your best photos with your price and address. On Instagram, add location tags and hashtags: #[YourCity]RealEstate #HomesForSale #[Neighborhood] #FSBO. On Nextdoor, post in your neighborhood — neighbors often know someone looking to move nearby.

4

Post on Craigslist

Go to craigslist.org → your city → Real Estate for Sale → Post. Free. Reaches investors and buyers who prefer off-MLS deals. Refresh every 48 hours to stay at the top of results.

5

Put up a yard sign with a QR code

A visible yard sign is still one of the most effective local tools — especially for capturing drive-by interest. Include your phone number and a QR code linking to your Zillow listing. You can order a professional FSBO sign from Amazon, Home Depot, or local print shops for $20–60.

6

Tell everyone you know

Text your contacts, post on your personal Facebook/Instagram, email your network. Include price, address, and 2–3 photos. Referrals and word-of-mouth are how a surprising number of FSBO sales close — especially in tight communities.

7

Host an open house

Schedule one weekend afternoon (Saturday or Sunday, 12–3pm is standard). Announce it on Zillow (there's an Open House section in your listing), Facebook, and Nextdoor 5–7 days in advance. Have a sign-in sheet — collect every visitor's name, email, and phone for follow-up.

$200 – $400 (one-time scan)

Allows buyers to walk through your home from anywhere. Critical for out-of-state buyers and investors who won't make an offer without seeing a home. Listings with 3D tours get significantly more saves and longer engagement time on Zillow.

→ Book a Matterport scan through matterport.com/service-providers or a local real estate photographer who offers it. The scan link embeds directly into Zillow.

Direct Mail to Neighbors

$0.50 – $1.50 per piece · 200–500 homes recommended

A postcard to the 200–500 homes nearest yours reaches the most motivated buyers — people who already love the neighborhood and may have friends or family wanting to move nearby. Direct mail outperformed digital in some studies for generating high-quality leads.

→ Use Wise Pelican or SendOutCards. Design a simple postcard with your best photo, price, and QR code. Mail to a 0.5-mile radius of your address.
Medium ROI

Google Search Ads

$10 – $50/day · $300 – $800 total budget

Targets people actively searching "[city] homes for sale" or "[zip code] real estate." High purchase intent. Works best when you have a property webpage (not just Zillow) to send traffic to. CPC in real estate averages $10–30+.

→ Use Google Ads. Target keywords: "[your zip] homes for sale", "[your city] [beds]bd house for sale". Set location targeting to your metro area. Requires a landing page — use Carrd or Wix to build a simple one-page property site ($10–20/mo).
Medium ROI

Zillow Premier Agent Ads

$150 – $500/month

Zillow allows sellers to boost listing visibility by purchasing Premier Agent placement. Your listing appears higher in zip code searches. Best for competitive areas where inventory is high and your listing needs more prominence.

→ Go to your Zillow listing → click Boost Your Listing. Set your zip code and budget. Run for 30 days and evaluate lead quality.
Medium ROI

Virtual Staging (AI)

$15 – $50 per photo

If rooms are empty or poorly furnished, AI virtual staging adds realistic furniture to photos — dramatically improving buyer perception. Services like REimagineHome or BoxBrownie produce results in minutes. California requires disclosure if used.

→ REimagineHome.ai or BoxBrownie.com. Upload your photo, select a style, download the result. Disclose "virtually staged" on all uses in California.

Step 1 — What to collect from the seller

Build a simple intake form (Google Form or Typeform works fine) that collects everything the VA needs to enter the listing without asking you a single question. Send this link to every seller before anything else happens.

Seller intake form — required fields
□ Full property address □ Asking price □ Property type (SFR/condo/etc.) □ Bedrooms + bathrooms (full/half) □ Square footage — living area □ Lot size (sq ft) □ Year built □ Garage type + capacity □ HOA yes/no + monthly amount + name □ Pool yes/no □ Occupancy status □ Annual property taxes □ Buyer's agent commission offered (%) □ Target close date □ Preferred showing instructions □ Any items included/excluded □ Known defects or disclosures □ Recent improvements (year + description) □ Seller contact: name, phone, email □ Photo upload (min 20, JPEG/PNG, landscape)

The AI listing agent in this system generates all of this automatically during the seller intake conversation and exports it in a format the VA can use directly.

Step 2 — VA standard operating procedure (SOP)

Give this SOP to every VA you hire. It's the exact sequence they follow for every listing. No guessing, no back-and-forth with you.

1
Receive the completed seller intake form
Check that all required fields are filled. If anything is missing (especially photos or address), email the seller using the template in the shared Google Drive folder before touching the MLS. Do not start a partial entry.
2
Download and organize photos
Create a folder in Google Drive: [Property Address] — [Date]. Download all seller-provided photos. Rename them in order: 01_exterior_front, 02_living_room, 03_kitchen, etc. Lead photo must be the best exterior shot. Check each photo is landscape orientation, well-lit, and free of clutter. Flag any that need replacement before uploading.
3
Log into the MLS with broker credentials
Use the shared login in the team password manager (1Password or Bitwarden). Never save credentials in a browser. If you get a two-factor prompt, use the authenticator app on your phone — do not ask the broker to send you the code every time; set this up properly on day one.
4
Create new listing — enter all fields
Select Add New Listing → Residential (or the correct property type). Work through every tab: Property Details, Price/Terms, Remarks, Photos, Open Houses. Use the intake form as your source of truth. Do not invent or estimate any field — if something is blank on the form, leave the MLS field blank or use the MLS default. The broker reviews before activation.
5
Paste the listing description into public and agent remarks
The AI listing agent generates two versions: Public Remarks (buyer-facing, no agent contact info, MLS character limit varies by MLS — typically 1,000–2,000 characters) and Private/Agent Remarks (showing instructions, lockbox info, contact preferences, buyer's agent compensation note). Paste each in the correct field. Check character count — trim if over limit.
6
Upload photos in correct order
Upload all photos from the organized folder. Confirm the lead photo (01_exterior_front) is set as the primary display image. Most MLS systems auto-sort by filename — verify the order after upload. Check the photo tab shows all images, no blanks or errors.
7
Save as draft — do NOT activate
Save the listing in Draft or Coming Soon status. Never activate a listing without broker review. Send the broker a message with: the MLS draft link, a checklist confirming all fields are entered, and a note of anything you couldn't complete or had questions about.
8
Broker reviews and activates
Broker logs in, spot-checks all fields, reviews photos and description, and clicks Activate. This takes about 5 minutes. The MLS listing goes live immediately. Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, and Trulia pick it up automatically within 24–48 hours — no additional action needed.
9
Confirm syndication within 48 hours
48 hours after activation, search the address on Zillow, Realtor.com, and Redfin. Confirm it appears with correct price, photos, and contact info. Screenshot confirmation and add to the property folder. If it doesn't appear, contact the MLS help desk — do not create a separate FSBO listing.
10
Notify the seller and deliver the publishing guide
Email the seller: "Your listing is live on the MLS. Here are your links: [Zillow] [Realtor.com] [Redfin]. Your next steps are in the attached publishing guide — follow the free marketing checklist to maximize exposure." Attach or link the publishing guide PDF. Log the completion date in the tracking spreadsheet.

Three non-negotiable rules for broker-VA MLS workflows
1. VA never activates. They build the draft. You activate. Every time. This takes you 5 minutes and keeps you compliant with MLS rules that require broker oversight of all submissions.
2. VA never communicates directly with buyers or agents. All inquiries go to you or the seller. The VA's job is data entry and logistics only — not representation.
3. Give the VA a dedicated MLS sub-login if your MLS supports it — not your personal broker login. This way you can revoke access instantly if needed and audit what was changed and when.
